Conceived by Joseph Grima and Kazys Varnelis, the New City Reader is an itinerant newspaper that reads cities. The New City Reader inquires into the changing role of the newspaper’s voice in the city. The newspaper is critical to shaping the modern city in a wide variety of ways. Literally the entirety of a newspaper, from the front page headlines to architectural criticism to urban boosterism to crime reportage to restaurant reviews impacts the city. But with the medium by which news is collected and disseminated so radically changing, how do the institutions formerly known as newspapers continue to affect the built landscape?

In emulation of a practice common in the nineteenth century, and still popular in parts of the world today, The New City Reader is designed to be posted in public for collective reading. Released regularly through a run, each issue of the The New City Reader is conceived of by a contributing group of made up architects, artists, designers, theorists, and researchers who will are chosen to bring their own particular expertise to their individual section.

Mounted on walls and read in public, the New City Reader is a form of writing in the streets. With the modern metropolis and mass literacy, reading and writing spread into the streets as advertisements announced sales and events, stores proclaimed their names and the goods they sold, while newspaper offices posted sections of papers outside of news offices to encourage people hurrying by to purchase the latest edition. The new technologies are transforming the media today are also transforming the way we read, particularly the way we read in public. People now stand like islands in a moving stream, gazing at portable media devices. Instead of reading texts side by side with others, they are reading text messages, checking their e-mail, looking up directions on a map, or checking in at their favorite social media site. This city of “augmented reality” purports to make us more connected, but actually makes us less connected than ever. If the New City Reader takes advantage of contemporary technology, it also turns back against our network culture, returning to the old practice of posting newspapers in public, to put our argument out into the city to be read, and encourage debate and discussion in the streets again.

The first New City Reader was launched in New York in 2010. The second New City Reader took place in Istanbul in 2012.

More New City Readers are in the works.
