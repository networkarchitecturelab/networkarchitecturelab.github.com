Internships

The Network Architecture Lab seeks applicants for its internship program on a rolling basis.

We are looking for interns unafraid to question the basic premises of architecture and to operate far beyond its boundaries, in territories more familiar to history, philosophy, economics, and religion. The Netlab is a research-based center aimed at understanding how digital technology, telecommunications, and changing social networks impact architecture, cities, and society. We do not design buildings.

Please explore this Web site in depth to understand what we do and how you might work with us. Please do not apply if you have not looked at these or are uninterested or methods of working. Most (but not all) of current research projects is listed under "Ongoing Projects."

Interns are expected to be self-starters and capable of independent work. We regret that all internships at present are unpaid, we do, however, give you full credit on any work you do.

Fluency in English is a must.

Responsibilities

support with long term projects and daily office tasks
interest in the Netlab's mission and agenda
strong writing and editing skills
strong communication skills
desire to learn about the daily operations of a research group
basic knowledge of html preferred
all interns are expected to be available for two months minimum.

Application to be submitted as a single PDF (8MB maximum) document including:

Cover letter
Resume/CV
Portfolio
Letters of recommendation (optional)

contact: info at networkarchitecturelab.org

Applicants with questions may also submit queries via the contact form on this site.

All positions demand significant time and resources from the Netlab, thus they must be highly selective and any projects that applicants want to engage in must be in keeping with our mission.

We are unable to help with visa or residency issues.
